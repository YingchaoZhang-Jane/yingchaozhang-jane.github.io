Yingchao Zhang, PhD

Visiting Professor, University of Cambridge
Assistant Professor, Durham University

I am an economist specialising in labour and personnel economics, entrepreneurship, and firm performance, with a focus on SMEs, ESG, and innovation. My research integrates applied microeconomics and experimental methods to study how firms grow, adapt, and respond to technological and sustainability challenges.

I am Investigator of a major research project on SME growth and regional development (£752,474). My work has been published in journals including Small Business Economics, with ongoing projects targeting leading outlets such as Management Science, Research Policy, and the Human Resource Management Journal.

Alongside my research, I am actively engaged in teaching at undergraduate, postgraduate, and executive levels, and I supervise dissertations across BSc, MSc, and DBA/PhD programmes.
